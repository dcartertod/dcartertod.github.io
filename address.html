Moved within precinct

If a voter has moved within the same precinct, they can vote, but should update their registration.

There may be a ? next to voter's name or the pollbook says 'inactive' or 'marked for address confirmation'.

  1. Officer asks voter to complete and sign a Voter Registration Application.
  2. Officer checks voter into the pollbook as normal.
  3. Officer places completed documents in Envelope #8.
  4. Voter casts ballot as normal in the precinct.

Do not enter voter's new address in pollbook.

Voter registered here; Voter moved to a different precinct on or after November 8, 2023

Voter is registered in this precinct, but voter moved to a different precinct within the same county/city AND congressional district. They vote here.

  1. Voter completes and signs a Voter Registration Application.
  2. Officer checks voter into the pollbook as normal.
  3. Voter casts ballot as normal in precinct where still registered.
  4. Officer places completed documents in Envelope #8.
Voter registered in different precinct; Voter moved to this precinct on or after November 8, 2023

Voter can vote in the precinct within the city/county and district where they are registered. They vote in that precinct.

Voter registered here; moved to different precinct from November 4, 2020 through November 7, 2023

Voter is registered in this precinct, but moved to a different precinct within the same county/city AND congressional district. They should complete the Affirmation of Eligibility.

  1. Officer fills out and initials Section A on the Affirmation of Eligibility form and checks Box C and Box 4.
  2. Voter fills out and signs Section B - Affirmation of Voter using their new address.
  3. Officer checks voter into the pollbook and indicates in the pollbook that a statement or oath was signed.
  4. Voter casts ballot as normal in precinct where still registered.
  5. Officer places completed documents in Envelope #8.
Voter registered in different precinct; moved to this precinct from November 4, 2020 through November 7, 2023

Voter can vote in the precinct within this city/county and district where they are registered with the Affirmation of Eligibility.

Provisional Ballot where they live

The voter can vote a Provisional Ballot in the precinct where they live.

Provisional Ballot in this precinct

The voter can vote a Provisional Ballot in this precinct.

Voter is no longer eligible to vote in Virginia.

  1. Ask voter to fill out a Cancellation Request form (ELECT 427(A)) or write and sign a note that:
    • states they have moved out of state;
    • asks to cancel their Virginia voter registration; and
    • includes their name, date of birth, and new address, (SSN optional).
  2. Place Cancellation Request or note in Envelope #8.

If voter insists they are registered and eligible to vote in the precinct, they may vote a Provisional Ballot.

Voter may vote a “Presidential-Only” Ballot

This is a presidential election AND voter moved out of state less than 30 days ago.

  1. Officer fills out and initials Section A on the Affirmation of Eligibility form and checks Box C and Box 3.
  2. Voter fills out and signs Section B – Affirmation of Voter using new address.
  3. Officer tells voter that the General Registrar will cancel the voter’s Virginia registration after this election
  4. Officer checks voter into the pollbook and indicates in the pollbook that a statement was signed
  5. Officer indicates in the pollbook that voter voted a presidential-only ballot
  6. Officer issues a presidential-only ballot
  7. Voter casts presidential-only ballot as normal in the precinct.
  8. Place Cancellation Request or note in Envelope #8.

If voter insists they are registered and eligible to vote in the precinct, they may vote a Provisional Ballot.

Polling Place Status

The address the voter provides orally or in writing must match the address in the pollbook

The provided address does NOT have to match the address on the ID.

  • If an address on an ID does not match the address on the pollbook, it does not make the ID unacceptable for proving voter identity.
  • Some acceptable forms of ID do not have an address.

A voter who moved out of town cannot vote in the town election but may be eligible to vote in non-town elections § 24.2-101; Qualified voter in a town.
